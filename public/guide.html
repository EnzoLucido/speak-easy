A Guide to Using SpeakEasy

To begin using SpeakEasy, simply press record. When you stop recording, SpeakEasy automatically analyzes your data.

Understanding What SpeakEasy Measures

Mean Pitch (F0)

How "high" or "low" your voice is, on average.

Pitch Standard Deviation

A measure of how much your pitch varies.

Harmonics-to-Noise Ratio (HNR)

The clarity of a voice. Lower scores indicate hoarseness or breathiness.

Pitch

How "high" or "low" your voice was at each moment in the recording.

Formants (F1, F2, F3)

These are resonant frequencies shaped by the position of your tongue and lips:

  • F1: Reflects how open your mouth is.
  • F2: Relates to where your tongue is in your mouth.
  • F3: Can reflect more nuanced vocal tract features, including lip rounding or speech clarity.

Tracking these can help with precise vocal adjustments, especially in gender-affirming voice training. However, it is important to remember that you must use the same words to compare changes.

Tips

Voice analysis is very susceptible to noise, so if you see something that looks like an outlier, it probably is!

Some words are naturally higher than others, so it can be useful to compare saying the same words to one another.
